WELCOME TO ASIA

There are so many exciting top destinations in Asia, deciding where to go can be difficult; but thats a good problem to have.

If planning your first big trip in Asia, consider a soft landing in one of these top destinations for first-time travelers.

1.Japan

Select sushi rolls from conveyor belt, walk among pastel pink cherry blossoms, and watch the sunrise over Mount Fuji-you are in the Land of the Rising Sun, after all.

2. Maldives

Who doesn't want to swing in a hammock over cerulean waters or falls asleep to the sound of lapping waves beneath an overwater bungalow? The Maldives faces the growing threat of global warming - if there was ever a time to make it to the remote island nation, it's now.

3.Thailand

Learn how to cook local curries in Chuang Mai, shop for souvenirs in Bangkok's maze-like malls and markets, or learn how to scuba dive in one of sports most popular destinations -Koh Tao. The "Land of Smiles" could never disappoint.

4. Korea

From impressive museums in the capital city of Seoul to the honeymoon-worthy beaches of Jeju Island, Korea is much more than the land of DIY barbecue, cult favorite beauty products, and K-pop.

5. Singapore

Singapore is a haven for the world's most serious shoppers and eaters alike. The city's safe streets and clean public transportation systems make it a breeze to navigate, while its hidden gem food halls, gleaming malls and impresive gardens make Singapore the perfect all-rounder for those looking for an exciting city break.

6. China

Take in the enormity of the great wall of China, view the terracota army defending Emperor Qin's tomb, or decide for yourself which Yangtze River Delta town really desrves that "Venice of the East" title.

7. Laos

Mountainous, landlocked Laos is as beloved for its natural beauty as its cultural heritage. Drive through the winding country side, relax at cafes in Vientiane, and admire historic Luang Prabang.

8. Taiwan

Shop shoulder-to-shoulder with locals at the night market; see artifacts from the Sung, Yuan, Ming and Ch'ing dynaties at the National Palace Museum; or ride the elevator to the top of Taipei Tower 101 for stunning overhead view of The Deautiful Isle.

9. Philippines

The Philippines feels out sync with the rest of Southeast Asia, and therein lies its charm. Set at a remove from the traditonal backpacker circuit that runs from Thailand to Vietnam, the Philippines feels more Ltin than "Oriental", more earthly than spiritual, and certainly less bound by the usal tourist rules

10. Myanmar

Myanmar, the former Burma, is one of the most magical places you'll ever go. Open to tourism just recently, Myanmar is full of hidden gems, without the crowds ypu'd see elsewhere.
